Botany Blog

Role: Frontend Developer

Please excuse the dust. This project is currently a work in progress.

Background

One of my favorite hobbies is to go outside and look at plants. Learning about the natural world and what is around us gives us a better appreciation and connection to the things that surround us. Discovering what is native to your area also gives us perspective on the profound impact humans have had on the natural world and what role we can play in order to protect what little shred of intact habitat remains. The distribution of plants fascinates me and we are able to learn a lot about many different facets of geological time based on their locations and growing habits.

Solution

Using React and the fetch API, I built a small blog about the plants I have seen and uploaded to iNaturalist. This is the main tool I use to learn what it is I am looking at by taking pictures and uploading it to their extensive database. It is an open-source project that provides robust way to programmatically access information for the benefit of researchers and hbbiest alike. My blog features my own observations that have been deemed research grade by others in the community that also use iNaturalist. The pictuures on the homepage are my own and the information provided on each plant page are from iNaturalist and Wikipedia.
